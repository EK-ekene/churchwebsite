Son of man, prophesy 2 - Rev Seth Frimpong

Greetings, seekers of truth! As we gather again, ponder this: What does it mean to prophesy to the "Son of Man"? In the depths of Ezekiel's vision, lies a mystery waiting to be unraveled. Join me in exploring its depths and unearthing the treasures of God's Word.

Ladies and gentlemen, brothers and sisters in Christ, it is a privilege to gather once again to delve into the Word of God and seek His wisdom. Last time, we explored the concept of prophesying to the "Son of Man," drawing inspiration from Ezekiel 37:1-6. Today, we continue our journey of understanding and delve into more scriptures that shed light on this profound theme. Let us turn to Ezekiel 37:1-6, and as we do, let us open our hearts to the guidance of the Holy Spirit.

I. Reviving the Dry Bones: Ezekiel 37:1-6
In these verses, the prophet Ezekiel is brought to a valley filled with dry bones. Through his obedience to God's command, he prophesies to these bones, and they come to life, symbolizing the restoration of Israel's spiritual vitality. This narrative teaches us that God's Word has the power to breathe life into the lifeless areas of our lives. We, too, are called to prophesy life into situations that seem dead, trusting that God's Word is a force of transformation.

II. Chosen Generation and Royal Priesthood: 1 Peter 2:9
The apostle Peter reminds us that we are a chosen generation, a royal priesthood, a holy nation. As children of God, we carry the responsibility to prophesy to the world by living lives that reflect our divine identity. Our words, actions, and character should declare God's praises, illuminating the darkness around us.

III. The Anointed Mission: Luke 4:18
Jesus declared in Luke 4:18 that the Spirit of the Lord was upon Him to proclaim good news to the poor, to bind up the brokenhearted, and to set the captives free. As followers of Christ, we are called to continue His mission. Our prophetic voice is one that stands against injustice, offers hope to the downtrodden, and shares the freedom found in Christ's redemption.

IV. Prophesy from the Mouth of Babes: Psalm 8:2
In Psalm 8:2, we're reminded of the potency of praise and worship. Even from the mouths of babes, God establishes His strength and silences the enemy. Our prophetic declarations can take the form of praise, glorifying God and silencing the doubts and fears that attempt to undermine our faith.

V. Taming the Tongue: James 3:3-6
James cautions us about the power of the tongue. Although small, the tongue has the potential to steer the course of our lives. To prophesy effectively, we must ensure that our words align with God's truth, for out of the abundance of the heart, the mouth speaks.

VI. Through the Valley: Psalm 23:4
"When I walk through the valley of the shadow of death, I will fear no evil, for you are with me." These words from Psalm 23:4 remind us that even in the darkest moments, we are not alone. Our prophetic journey might lead us through valleys, but God's presence and promises provide comfort and courage.

VII. Under His Wings: Psalm 91:5, Psalm 3:3
Psalm 91:5 speaks of finding refuge under the wings of the Almighty. In times of trouble, we can prophesy victory over our circumstances, trusting that God's protection is our shield. Psalm 3:3 further reinforces this truth, as it proclaims that God is our shield, our glory, and the lifter of our heads.

Dear friends, as we continue to explore the theme of prophesying to the Son of Man, let us remember that our lives are a prophetic declaration of God's grace, love, and power. Just as Ezekiel brought life to dry bones and Jesus fulfilled His mission on Earth, we too are called to prophesy life, hope, and transformation in the world around us. May we be vessels of His truth, conduits of His love, and bearers of His light. Let us go forth and proclaim His goodness through our words and deeds. God bless you all.
